## Concept Summary

Reef Bloom is a stewardship simulation where the player cultivates a living coral reef ecosystem. There is no failure state — the player is a caretaker, not a controller. Chaos emerges from systems; the reef grows in complexity through observation, placement, and patience rather than combat or grinding.

**Foundational Design Principles:**
- All species must be real biological organisms (fish, coral, mammals, invertebrates)
- Stylization is allowed; fictional biology is not
- The game has no permanent failure states
- The player is a steward, not a controller
- Depth of behavior is preferred over quantity of content
- Chaos emerges from systems; it is not forced

---

## Core Gameplay Loop

1. **Earn Bubble Energy (BE)** — Generated passively by placed coral; bonus BE from idle observation streaks
2. **Spend BE** — Place new coral tiles or hatch fish
3. **Attract & observe** — Fish are drawn to coral, light, and other fish; behavior unfolds organically
4. **Build balance** — Harmony rises as the reef becomes more balanced and diverse
5. **Level up** — Reaching new player levels unlocks new species; complexity compounds

## Core Metrics

### Bubble Energy (BE)
The primary resource and currency.

- **Generated by:** Coral (passively, per hour based on tier)
- **Bonus BE from:** Idle observation streak — if the player watches without touching for a set duration, a burst of bonus BE is awarded. Rewards stillness and watching.
- **Spent on:** Placing coral, hatching fish
- **Rule:** BE is never removed as punishment

| Tier | Symbol | Coral BE/hr | Coral Cost | Fish Cost |
|------|--------|-------------|------------|-----------|
| Common | 🟦 | 15 | 1 | 3 |
| Uncommon | 🟩 | 30 | 5 | 5 |
| Rare | 🟥 | 45 | 10 | 12 |

### Harmony
Represents ecosystem balance — NOT an XP system.

- **Range:** 0–100
- **What raises it:** Having a good balance of species, coral variety, and fish — no single trigger, it reflects the overall health and diversity of the reef
- **Behavior:**
  - Only provides positive feedback
  - Never permanently decreases
  - Does NOT directly unlock species
- **Role:** A "feel good" metric — shows the player their reef is thriving
- **Visual effect:** The entire reef's color saturation scales with Harmony. At 100, colors are rich and vibrant. As Harmony drops toward 0, the palette desaturates — the world looks washed out. This is the primary way the player feels Harmony, not just reads it.

### Player Level
An explicit level number visible to the player.

- Species unlock at specific level thresholds
- Only two species unlock before Level 3
- Unlocks happen via: time, observation, coral placement, behavior triggers
- Unlocks do NOT happen via: combat, XP grinding, ads, or gacha mechanics
- Species may appear (be seen by the player) before they are unlockable — creates discovery anticipation

## Grid & World Layout

### Main Reef Grid
- **Size:** 10×10 tiles
- **Display:** Always fully visible on screen — no panning or scrolling
- **Fish swim on two layers:**
  - **Layer A** — blocked by ALL coral
  - **Layer B** — blocked by TALL coral only

### Off-Grid Areas (non-editable)
- **Rocky Outcrop** contains:
  - Fish Nest — where fish are hatched
  - Bubbles' Charging Dock — Bubbles returns here when idle
- Off-grid areas are not counted as tiles and cannot be edited by the player

---

## World & Visual Layout

- **Environment:** Coral reef, underwater, landscape orientation
- **Camera:** Fixed — the entire reef is visible at once
- **Depth Layers (2.5D):**

| Layer | Name | Contents |
|-------|------|----------|
| 0 | Background | Distant ocean haze, caustic light rays from surface |
| 1 | Mid-back | Rock formations, large coral structures, Rocky Outcrop |
| 2 | Main plane | Gameplay grid — coral placement, fish Layer A |
| 3 | Upper plane | Fish Layer B |
| 4 | Foreground | Floating particles, seaweed tendrils, small ambient details |
| 5 | UI Overlay | HUD (BE counter, Harmony bar, level), placement menu |

---

## Visual Style

- **Aesthetic:** Flat / minimal — strong silhouettes, limited palette per species, premium and calm feel
- **Species design:** Iconic not realistic — readable at small tile sizes, distinctive silhouettes
- **UI:** Blends seamlessly with world — no heavy chrome or frames
- **Color Palette:** Warm reef tones — teals, corals, sandy yellows, deep blues; each species has a tight 3–4 color palette
- **Lighting:** Caustic light rays from surface; subtle ambient glow; achieved via layer-level PixiJS filters
- **Animation style:** Minimal and purposeful — gentle idle loops, no jank

### 2.5D Technique Approach
- One PixiJS Container per depth layer, z-ordered as a stack
- Sprites scaled slightly by layer (further = smaller) to simulate depth
- Soft blur / color grade filter on background layers for depth-of-field
- Drop shadows on mid-plane elements to anchor them to the grid
- No scrolling — all layers are fixed-position

## Technical Stack

| Concern | Choice | Notes |
|---------|--------|-------|
| Renderer | **PixiJS v8** | WebGL layering, excellent iPad/Safari perf |
| Fish AI | Custom boids-lite steering | No physics engine needed |
| BE economy | Vanilla JS tick system | Simple accumulator |
| Harmony | Vanilla JS | Computed from reef state snapshot |
| Idle streak | Vanilla JS inactivity timer | Tracks last touch event |
| State management | Vanilla JS modules | Keep simple for prototype |
| Persistence | **localStorage** | Works offline, no server needed; fine for prototype and final app |
| Bubbles dialogue | Pre-authored script | Static string array, authored with Claude assistance |
| Asset pipeline | free-tex-packer or TexturePacker | Sprite atlases per layer |
| iPad wrapper | **Capacitor** | WKWebView, actively maintained |
| Build tool | **Vite** | Fast dev server + optimized builds |
| Language | JavaScript (ES2022+) | TypeScript optional — decide before scaling |

### Why PixiJS for 2.5D
- Container-based layer system maps directly to the depth layer design
- Hardware-accelerated WebGL on iPad/Safari
- Per-layer filters: blur, color matrix, light effects
- Strong WKWebView support via Capacitor

## Prototype Scope (MVP)

The first playable prototype must prove out:

- [ ] 10×10 grid renders correctly in landscape, fully visible
- [ ] At least one coral can be placed by tapping an empty tile
- [ ] BE accumulates passively from placed coral
- [ ] BE can be spent to place new coral
- [ ] At least one fish species swims with idle behavior on Layer A/B
- [ ] Fish avoid coral correctly per layer rules
- [ ] Idle observation streak triggers a BE bonus burst
- [ ] 2.5D depth layers read clearly at a glance
- [ ] Touch input works on iPad (tap to select, tap to place)
- [ ] 60fps on iPad hardware

**Explicitly out of scope for prototype:**
- Audio and SFX
- Bubbles AI / dialogue system
- Harmony metric
- Player level system and unlocks
- Off-grid Rocky Outcrop / Fish Nest
- Menus, save/load, settings
- Rare (🟥) and uncommon (🟩) species
- Persistence (localStorage)
